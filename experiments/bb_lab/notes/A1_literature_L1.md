# A1 — Literature lane L1: repeated-root / non-semisimple abelian code distance theory

Date: 2026-06-09. Phase 1, lane L1 of the analytic-bound program.
Researcher findings adversarially verified at source; all corrections
from the verifier are applied inline below. Quotes are the
**verifier's** verified verbatim extracts, not the researcher's
originals.

**Lane verdict:** The classical repeated-root literature contains an
exact, non-semisimple, valuation-based distance theorem (CMS 1991) —
but only univariate. Its proof mechanism (project a codeword at its
(x^n̄−1)-adic depth t, weight factorizes as ≥ P_t · d̄_t) is precisely
the shape Track A wants to generalize to the bivariate radical
filtration of F₂[Z₄ × Z₂], and **no published multivariate
generalization exists**: the PIR/PIGA characterizations (Cazaran–Kelarev,
Fisher–Sehgal) prove that every published generator-polynomial distance
line (CMS, Dinh/Özadam–Özbudak constacyclic, Martínez-Moro–Rúa
multivariable-PIR, Jitman–Ling–Liu–Xie PIGA) stops structurally short
of gross's two-repeated-axes, non-PIR ambient F₂[Z₁₂ × Z₆]. The
ingredients for a Track A theorem are all published and verified
(univariate weight ladders, exact tensor-monomial distances in
F₂[Z₄ × Z₂], the Choosuwan local decomposition of gross's exact
algebra type, and an exact h=2 Plotkin/van Lint cover-distance theorem
for Track B). Two cautions survive verification: the hoped-for
Drensky–Lakatos shortcut to radical-power minimum weights of
F₂[Z₄ × Z₂] is **REFUTED** (their ambient is elementary-abelian only),
and the "no analytic BB distance lower bound exists anywhere" novelty
claim must be narrowed — Wang–Pryadko 2022 gives weak analytic GB-code
lower bounds applicable to coprime BB codes.

## Verified findings

### CMS 1991 Theorem 1 — exact distance of repeated-root cyclic codes (CONFIRMED)

**Claim (corrections applied).** CMS 1991 Theorem 1 gives an EXACT
formula (not just a bound) for the minimum distance of any q-ary
repeated-root cyclic code C of length n = p^δ·n̄ (gcd(p, n̄) = 1):
d_min(C) = P_t̂ · d_min(C̄_t̂) for some t̂ in an explicit small set T,
where C̄_t are simple-root cyclic codes of length n̄ (generated by the
irreducible factors of g(x) of multiplicity > t) and P_t is a purely
combinatorial weight factor. Combined with Lemma 1
(d_min(C) ≤ P_t · d_min(C̄_t) for ALL t), this yields
d_min(C) = min_{t ∈ T} P_t · d_min(C̄_t). Edge-case conventions
(verifier addition): d_min(C̄_t) = ∞ when ḡ_t = x^n̄ − 1, and
d_min(C̄_t) = 1 when ḡ_t = 1. The lower-bound direction is a
valuation-determined per-codeword bound — for c(x) = (x^n̄−1)^t v(x)
with (x^n̄−1) ∤ v(x) and t̂ = min{t̄ ∈ T | t̄ ≥ t}, eqs.
(20)/(21)/(25)/(27) give w_H(c) ≥ P_t̂ · d_min(C̄_t̂). The phrase
"multiplicative transfer through the radical filtration" is the
researcher's gloss, not paper language, but the verifier checked it is
mathematically faithful to the Lemma 2 mechanism.

**Source.** Castagnoli, Massey, Schoeller, von Seemann, "On
Repeated-Root Cyclic Codes", IEEE Trans. Inform. Theory 37(2):337–342,
1991; DOI 10.1109/18.75249. Theorem 1 (eq. 28), p. 340; Lemma 1 (eq. 9)
and the pre-Lemma-2 sentence, p. 339. Free scan at the ETH Massey
archive (BI433.pdf); OCR-normalized.

**Verified quote.**
> "Theorem 1: Let C be a q-ary repeated-root cyclic code of blocklength
> n = p^δ n̄ with p the characteristic of GF(q), δ ≥ 1 and
> gcd(p, n̄) = 1. Then d_min(C) = P_t̂ · d_min(C̄_t̂) (28) for some
> t̂ ∈ T, where T is the set defined following (12)." — and (p. 339,
> *preceding* Theorem 1 in document order): "We shall now prove that the
> minimum distance of the repeated-root cyclic code C is equal to the
> smallest of the expressions P_t · d_min(C̄_t) on the right of (9) by
> proving that there exists a minimum weight nonzero code polynomial in
> C of the form (10) for some t̂ ∈ T." — and "Lemma 1: The minimum
> distance d_min(C) of the repeated-root cyclic code C defined above
> satisfies d_min(C) ≤ P_t · d_min(C̄_t), for all
> t ∈ {0, 1, ···, p^δ − 1}. (9)"

**Applicability to gross.** Does NOT apply as stated: CMS requires G
cyclic (univariate). Gross's G = Z₁₂ × Z₆ is bicyclic with BOTH
coordinates carrying repeated roots over F₂ (4 | 12 and 2 | 6). The
hypothesis blocking direct use is univariateness, not semisimplicity —
CMS is genuinely non-semisimple prior art.

**Feeds.** Track A: this is the univariate prototype theorem; the
Lemma 2 valuation mechanism is the one to imitate with the radical
filtration of F₂[Z₄ × Z₂].

### CMS 1991 Lemma 1 + the P_t ladder (CONFIRMED)

**Claim (corrections applied).** CMS define C̄_t and the weight factor
P_t = ∏_i (t_i + 1) over the radix-p digits t_i of t (= w_H((x−1)^t);
"multiplicity weight" is the researcher's label — the paper leaves P_t
unnamed), prove d_min(C) ≤ P_t · d_min(C̄_t) for all 0 ≤ t ≤ p^δ − 1
(Lemma 1), and reduce the minimum to the set T = {0} ∪ {t per (13a)}
with P_t = p^(j−1)(r+1). For p = 2 the ladder is P_t ∈ {1, 2, 4, …, 2^δ}
(independently re-derived by the verifier from (13b)): the candidate
multiplicative factors m(A) in Track A's bound are powers of 2. The
claim does not assert CMS applies to the bivariate BB algebra — only
the ladder transfers.

**Source.** Same paper; Section III, eqs. (7)–(13c) and Lemma 1,
pp. 338–339.

**Verified quote.**
> "The generator of C̄_t, ḡ_t(x), is defined as the product of those
> irreducible factors m_i(x) of g(x) that occur with multiplicity
> e_i > t in g(x). [...] w_H((x − 1)^t) = P_t, (7) where
> P_t = ∏_i (t_i + 1), (8) and where the t_i's are the coefficients of
> the radix-p expansion of t. [...] The set T of values of t < p^δ
> satisfying (12) consists of t = 0 yielding P_0 = 1 and the values
> t = (p − 1)p^{δ−1} + ··· + (p − 1)p^{δ−(j−1)} + r p^{δ−j} (13a)
> yielding P_t = p^{j−1} · (r + 1), (13b) where j ∈ {1, ···, δ} and
> r ∈ {1, ···, p − 1}. (13c)"

**Applicability to gross.** Univariate-only blocker as above. The
structure transfers: the radical filtration of F₂[Z₄ × Z₂] has
nilpotency index (4−1)+(2−1)+1 = 5, and candidate m(A) values would be
min weights of radical powers of F₂[Z₄ × Z₂] — NOT in published closed
form (open question; the Drensky–Lakatos hope is refuted below).

**Feeds.** Track A: exact univariate prototype of the Loewy-layer
weight factor m(A).

### Generalized van Lint theorem — exact h=2 cover distance over char 2 (CONFIRMED-WITH-CORRECTIONS)

**Claim (corrections applied).** Over F_q with q a power of 2 and n
odd, the repeated-root cyclic code of length 2n generated by
g₁(x)² g₂(x) is permutation-equivalent to the Plotkin sum
{(u | u+v)} of C₁ = ⟨g₁⟩ and C₂ = ⟨g₁g₂⟩, with minimum distance
EXACTLY d = min{2 d(C₁), d(C₂)}. **Corrections:** the citing paper has
THREE authors — Hao Chen, **Conghui Xie**, Cunsheng Ding (the
researcher omitted Xie). "Classical" strictly applies only to van
Lint's 1991 binary original (IEEE TIT 37(2):343–345); the q-ary
generalized version is a 2023 Chen–Ding preprint result (their ref
[5]), restated as Theorem 2.1 of arXiv:2402.02853. The "h=2 cover"
framing is the researcher's gloss but is mathematically faithful
(char 2, x^{2n}+1 = (x^n+1)², Z_{2n} → Z_n degree-2 quotient). The
paper itself warns the conclusion may fail for odd q.

**Source.** Chen, Xie, Ding, "Repeated-Root Cyclic Codes with Optimal
Parameters or Best Parameters Known", arXiv:2402.02853, Theorem 2.1,
Section 2. Original: J. H. van Lint, "Repeated-root cyclic codes",
IEEE TIT 37(2):343–345, 1991.

**Verified quote.**
> "Theorem 2.1 (The generalized van Lint theorem [5]). Let q be a power
> of 2 and n be an odd positive integer. Let C_1 ⊆ F_q^n be a cyclic
> code with generator polynomial g_1(x) ∈ F_q[x] and C_2 ⊆ F_q^n be a
> cyclic code generated by the polynomial g_1(x)g_2(x) ∈ F_q[x], where
> g_2(x) is a divisor of x^n + 1. Then the code Plotkin(C_1, C_2) is
> permutation-equivalent to the repeated-root cyclic code C(g_1, g_2)
> of length 2n generated by the polynomial g_1(x)^2 g_2(x). In
> addition, the cyclic code C(C_1, C_2) has [...] minimum distance
> d(C(C_1, C_2)) = min{2d(C_1), d(C_2)}."

**Applicability to gross.** Applies verbatim only to single classical
cyclic blocks of length 2n, n odd. For gross one would need iterated
versions (length 4·odd: handled by CMS directly) and a bivariate
version (not in literature). The Plotkin-decomposition mechanism
(splitting F₂[G] along a free Z₂-action) is directly available for
gross's y³- or x⁶-translation.

**Feeds.** Track B: exact classical analogue of the h=2 BB cover step
in char 2, proved WITHOUT an SRB-style transfer map p∘τ = h·I (which
dies mod 2) — strong precedent that the even-h obstruction in HANDOFF
6k is a proof-technique artifact. Also Track A (composing van Lint at
the Z₂ factor with CMS at the Z₄ factor).

### Özadam–Özbudak — complete univariate constacyclic distance ladder (CONFIRMED)

**Claim (corrections applied).** Complete closed-form Hamming distance
for all constacyclic codes ⟨(x^n + γ)^i⟩ of length n·p^s over F_q
(x^n + γ irreducible): d = 2 for 1 ≤ i ≤ p^{s−1}; d = β + 2 on the β
ranges; d = (τ+1)p^k on the upper ranges. The theorem carries NO odd-p
hypothesis (verifier re-derived: the "p odd" assumption enters only at
Lemma 3.8 onward), so for p = 2, n = 1, γ = 1 it gives the exact
minimum weights of every ideal — every radical power — of
F₂[Z_{2^s}], corroborated by the paper's own char-2 Example 5.1.
"Generalizing MCJ 1973" is researcher-supplied but fair (the paper
frames itself as generalizing Dinh 2008 via Remark 3.7, with MCJ 1973
as the underlying weight-retaining tool). The "Loewy-layer ingredients
for m(A)" clause is program-internal framing resting on correctly
supported content.

**Source.** Özadam, Özbudak, "Two generalizations on the minimum
Hamming distance of repeated-root constacyclic codes",
arXiv:0906.4008, Theorem 3.6 and Corollary 3.5, Section 3 (p. 9).

**Verified quote.**
> "Theorem 3.6. [...] Suppose that x^n + γ ∈ F_q[x] is irreducible.
> Then the λ-cyclic codes over F_q, of length np^s, are of the form
> C[i] = ⟨(x^n + γ)^i⟩, where 0 ≤ i ≤ p^s and λ = −γ^{p^s}. [...] the
> minimum Hamming distance of C[i] is given by d_H(C[i]) = { 2, if
> 1 ≤ i ≤ p^{s−1}; β + 2, if βp^{s−1} + 1 ≤ i ≤ (β + 1)p^{s−1} where
> 1 ≤ β ≤ p − 2; (τ + 1)p^k, if p^s − p^{s−k} + (τ − 1)p^{s−k−1} + 1
> ≤ i ≤ p^s − p^{s−k} + τp^{s−k−1} where 1 ≤ τ ≤ p − 1 and
> 1 ≤ k ≤ s − 1 }."

**Applicability to gross.** Univariate only, but applies to each cyclic
factor of gross's G₂ = Z₄ × Z₂ separately: F₂[Z₄] has
d(⟨(x−1)¹⟩) = d(⟨(x−1)²⟩) = 2, d(⟨(x−1)³⟩) = 4; F₂[Z₂] has
d(⟨(y−1)⟩) = 2. The blocker is multivariateness, not semisimplicity.

**Feeds.** Track A: the per-axis weight atoms from which any
multivariate m(A) factor must be assembled.

### Monomial-like codes — exact tensor distances in F₂[Z₄ × Z₂]-type ambients (CONFIRMED)

**Claim (corrections applied).** For the modular abelian p-group
algebra R = F_{p^a}[x₁,…,x_n]/(x₁^{p^{s₁}}−1, …, x_n^{p^{s_n}}−1)
(Sylow-p arbitrary, includes Z₄ × Z₂), every monomial-like code
C = ⟨(x₁−1)^{i₁} ⋯ (x_n−1)^{i_n}⟩ is the tensor product of the
univariate codes, and d_H(C) = ∏_j d_H(C_{x_j}) with factors given by
the Dinh / Özadam–Özbudak formula. Scope (verified as conservatively
drawn): only principal ideals generated by a single monomial — not
general ideals, not radical powers (sums of monomial-like ideals), and
no semisimple factor in the ambient (Lemma 2.1 makes R local).
Verifier notes: Theorem 3.4 is actually MORE general (arbitrary
lengths r_j under a divisibility hypothesis); Theorem 3.4 requires all
i_j positive, so exponent vectors with some i_j = 0 are a (trivially
extendable) edge case; the result is peer-reviewed published as "On a
class of repeated-root monomial-like abelian codes" (J. Algebra Comb.
Discrete Struct. Appl., 2015).

**Source.** Martínez-Moro, Özadam, Özbudak, Szabo, "Monomial-like
codes", arXiv:1003.3386, Theorems 3.4 and 3.5, Section 3 (p. 7).

**Verified quote.**
> "Theorem 3.5. Let C = ⟨(x_1 − 1)^{i_1} · · · (x_n − 1)^{i_n}⟩ ⊂ R.
> Let R_{x_j} = F_q[x_j]/⟨x^{p^{s_j}} − 1⟩ and
> C_{x_j} = ⟨(x_j − 1)^{i_j}⟩ ⊂ R_{x_j}. Then
> d_H(C) = ∏_{j=1}^{n} d_H(C_{x_j}), where d_H(C_{x_j}) is as given in
> [2, Theorem 6.4] and [14, Theorem 1]."

([2] = Dinh 2008, [14] = Özadam–Özbudak 2009 — attribution verified.)

**Applicability to gross.** Ambient covers gross's G₂ = Z₄ × Z₂
exactly (p=2, s=(2,1)) but NOT the full gross algebra
F₂[G_odd × G₂] (no semisimple factor allowed), and BB kernels
ker(M_A) are not monomial-like ideals. An ingredient/calibration, not
a theorem about BB codes. E.g. ⟨(x−1)³(y−1)⟩ has d = 4·2 = 8 in
F₂[Z₄ × Z₂].

**Feeds.** Track A: exact published values any m(A) read off the
radical filtration of F₂[G₂] must calibrate against. Also corrects the
earlier Cv1_literature.md note that no closed-form min-weight machinery
exists for non-elementary 2-Sylow — it exists for single-monomial
ideals.

### PIGA definition and characterization — gross is outside all PIGA prior art (CONFIRMED-WITH-CORRECTIONS)

**Claim (corrections applied).** F_{p^k}[G] is a PIGA iff its ideals
are singly generated; for G = A × B with A an **abelian** group of odd
order and B a nontrivial **abelian** 2-group (the researcher dropped
the "abelian" qualifiers — immaterial here since Z₁₂ × Z₆ is abelian),
F_{2^k}[A × B] is a PIGA iff B is cyclic (Fisher–Sehgal 1976). Hence
F₂[Z₁₂ × Z₆] (B = Z₄ × Z₂ non-cyclic) is a non-PIGA, and the
PIGA-scoped Jitman–Ling–Liu–Xie 2013 theorems are inapplicable to
gross (researcher's inference, tautologically valid given the verified
non-PIGA status). Scope precision (verifier): the paper's "not well
studied" sentence is scoped to *characterization and enumeration of
self-dual* abelian codes in non-PIGAs; the paper treats only self-dual
characterization/enumeration (no distance theory) for B = Z₂ × Z_{2^s}.

**Source.** Choosuwan, Jitman, Udomkavanich, "Self-Dual Abelian Codes
in some Non-Principal Ideal Group Algebras", arXiv:1609.03038,
Section 1 (p. 2) and Sections 2.1–2.2 (pp. 3–4); [12] = Fisher–Sehgal,
"Principal ideal group rings", Comm. Algebra 4:319–325, 1976.

**Verified quote.**
> "The group algebra F_{p^k}[G] is called a principal ideal group
> algebra (PIGA) if its ideals are generated by a single element."
> [...] "In this case, F_{2^k}[A × B] is a PIGA if and only if
> B = Z_{2^s} is a cyclic group (see [12]). Equivalently,
> F_{2^k}[A × B] is a non-PIGA if and only if B is non-cyclic." [...]
> "To the best of our knowledge, the characterization and enumeration
> of self-dual abelian codes in non-principal ideal group algebras
> (non-PIGAs) have not been well studied."

**Applicability to gross.** Directly applicable as an
impossibility-of-prior-art statement: gross's 2-Sylow Z₄ × Z₂ is
non-cyclic, so F₂[Z₁₂ × Z₆] is a non-PIGA — exactly the regime the
literature declares under-studied.

**Feeds.** Track A: de-blocks the lane from the retracted Jitman–Ling
over-claim; any Track A theorem on F₂[Z₁₂ × Z₆] is outside the JL

### Berman–Charpin — radical powers of elementary-abelian group algebras are RM codes (CONFIRMED-WITH-CORRECTIONS)

**Claim (corrections applied).** Berman (1967) / Charpin (1988),
restated with a new proof by Andriatahiny: the p-ary Reed–Muller code
of order ν and length p^m equals M^{m(p−1)−ν}, M the radical of
F_p[(Z_p)^m]. **Corrections:** (1) "consequently the minimum weights of
ALL radical powers are known exactly (= GRM distances)" is the
researcher's inference, NOT stated in the paper (which never discusses
minimum weights); it is sound only combined with the classical
Kasami–Lin–Peterson GRM distance formula (the paper's ref [8]), and
"ALL" is justified since Theorem 3.6 covers every power. (2) The
blanket "no analogous closed form for non-elementary abelian p-groups"
over-reaches: for CYCLIC p-groups Z_{p^s} the closed form IS published
(CMS 1991 / Özadam–Özbudak). Correct phrasing: no closed form is
published for **non-cyclic, non-elementary-abelian** p-groups — which
includes the case that matters, Z₄ × Z₂. Supporting: the paper's
Theorem 5.1 shows the identification specifically fails beyond the
elementary-abelian/prime-field setting.

**Source.** Andriatahiny, "The Generalized Reed-Muller codes and the
radical powers of a modular algebra", arXiv:1601.07633, Theorem 3.6
(Section 3, p. 8); attributions: Berman, Kibernetika 3(1):31–39, 1967;
Charpin, Comm. Algebra 16:2231–2246, 1988 (bibliography verified).

**Verified quote.**
> "3.6 Theorem (Berman-Charpin). Let C_ν(m, p) be the Reed-Muller code
> of length p^m and of order ν (0 ≤ ν ≤ m(p − 1)) over the prime field
> F_p and M the radical of F_p[X_1, ..., X_m] / (X_1^p − 1, ...,
> X_m^p − 1). Then C_ν(m, p) = M^{m(p−1)−ν}."

**Applicability to gross.** Not directly applicable: G₂ = Z₄ × Z₂ is
not elementary abelian (blocking hypothesis: exponent p).

**Feeds.** Track A: establishes "min weight of radical powers" as a

### Cazaran–Kelarev PIR characterization — gross's ambient is not a PIR (CONFIRMED-WITH-CORRECTIONS)

**Claim (corrections applied).** The multivariate ambient
F_q[X₁,…,X_r]/(t₁(X₁),…,t_r(X_r)) over a finite field is a PIR iff at
most one t̄_i fails to be square-free (conditions strictly on the
residue-field reductions t̄_i — the overlines were dropped in the
researcher's transcription; immaterial over F₂ where reduction is the
identity). Over F₂ both x¹² − 1 = (x³−1)⁴ and y⁶ − 1 = (y³−1)² are
non-square-free, so F₂[x,y]/(x¹²−1, y⁶−1) is NOT a PIR (verifier
re-derived). **Correction:** the researcher's conclusion that this is
"the hard boundary of ALL published multivariate distance theory" is
an extrapolation beyond the source — what the paper establishes is the
PIR boundary for its own PIPQR machinery (Section 3 assumes PIR, hence
exactly one repeated axis; the distance result Proposition 2 sits under
that hypothesis), and it notes the non-PIR repeated-root case was
treated in [17] via Canonical Generating Systems with a "not as
satisfactory" description. The narrower, defensible statement: the
Martínez-Moro–Rúa-line principal-generation/distance machinery is
unavailable for gross's ambient.

**Source.** Martínez-Moro, Piñera-Nicolás, Rúa, "Multivariable codes in
principal ideal polynomial quotient rings...", arXiv:1709.05466,
Theorem 1, Section 2; characterization attributed to [4] =
Cazaran–Kelarev, "On finite principal ideal rings", Acta Math. Univ.
Comenianae LXVIII:77–84, 1999 (bibliography verified).

**Verified quote.**
> "Theorem 1. The ring R = R[X_1, . . . , X_r]/⟨t_1(X_1), . . . ,

**Verifier verdict: REFUTED.** The source exists with exact
bibliographic details (Drensky–Lakatos, "Monomial ideals, group

## Implications for Phase 2

**Nothing kills a planned track.** No impossibility theorem was found;
the asymptotic-badness results (CMS Thms 2–3, JLLX) concern δ → ∞
and/or PIGA-scoped single blocks and have no force at gross's fixed
small δ.

**Preemption status (Track A): not preempted, but the novelty claim
needs narrowing and two due-diligence checks.**

- The two-repeated-axes (non-PIR, non-PIGA) distance problem is
  untouched in the classical literature — verified across CMS,
  Dinh/Özadam–Özbudak, monomial-like, MM-Rúa, JLLX, and the BB
  literature. F₂[Z₁₂ × Z₆] is verifiably non-PIGA (Fisher–Sehgal) and
  non-PIR (Cazaran–Kelarev) — both inapplicability certificates are
  CONFIRMED.
- BUT: Wang–Pryadko 2022 (arXiv:2203.17216) provides weak analytic
  lower bounds for GB codes covering the coprime BB regime. Phase 2
  must cite and distinguish it, and must check Rowshan 2601.01137 at
  the body level (its abstract claims more about coprime-BB distance
  than its theorems appear to deliver).
- Must-fetch before any published novelty claim: MM-Rúa DCC 2007
  theorem statements (expected PIR-confined, but unverified at
  source).

**Enablement (the lane's main yield):**

- **Track A** gets a complete toolkit: the CMS Lemma 2 valuation
  mechanism as proof template (CONFIRMED exact theorem, not just a
  bound); the P_t = powers-of-2 ladder; the per-axis weight atoms of
  F₂[Z₄] and F₂[Z₂] (Özadam–Özbudak, no odd-p restriction —
  CONFIRMED); exact tensor-monomial distances
  d(⟨(x−1)^a(y−1)^b⟩) = d_x(a)·d_y(b) in F₂[Z₄ × Z₂] as calibration
  targets for any m(A) (monomial-like codes — CONFIRMED); and the
  Choosuwan decomposition F₂[Z₁₂ × Z₆] ≅ (F₂+uF₂)[Z₄] ×
  ((F₄+uF₄)[Z₄])⁴ as canonical coordinates (CONFIRMED, instantiation
  re-derived). Open question 3 — re-proving CMS's lower-bound half
  against the bivariate filtration with tensor weights as the
  P-ladder — is confirmed as genuinely open territory: no obstacle, no
  precedent.
- **Track B** gets the generalized van Lint theorem (CONFIRMED, with
  the Chen–**Xie**–Ding author correction): an exact h=2,
  characteristic-2 cover-distance equality d = min{2d(C₁), d(C₂)}
  proved by Plotkin decomposition, bypassing the p∘τ = h·I mechanism
  entirely. This is the strongest available evidence that the even-h
  obstruction (HANDOFF 6k) is an artifact of the SRB proof technique,
  and the Plotkin mechanism along a free Z₂-action is the natural
  starting point for the BB 2-cover transfer.
- **Tracks C/D**: no direct inputs from this lane beyond the novelty
  framing (k-theory of BB codes solved, tight d-theory open
  everywhere).

**Losses relative to the researcher's draft:** the Drensky–Lakatos

(elementary-abelian ambient only) — Track A should plan to derive the
