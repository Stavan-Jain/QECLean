# T3R2.4 — Bravyi-table consistency check (conditional Jacobson bound)

This document records what the **conditional** Jacobson-radical bound

> For non-degenerate BB codes,
> `d_X(BB(G, A, B)) ≥ Σ_{O ∈ V_A ∩ V_B} |O| · min(μ_O(A), μ_O(B))`

says about the five Bravyi-table reference instances. The
"non-degenerate" qualifier in the spec is

    NON-DEGENERATE :≡  ⟨supp(A)⟩ = G   AND   ⟨supp(B)⟩ = G

i.e., each polynomial's support generates the full group as an
additive subgroup.

---

## 1. The big surprise

**Under the spec's strict non-degeneracy definition, ALL 5 Bravyi
instances are degenerate.** Each has `[G : ⟨supp(A)⟩] = 3 = [G :
⟨supp(B)⟩]`. They are therefore **out of scope** for the conditional
conjecture — it makes no claim about them.

The reason is structural: every Bravyi polynomial is of the form
`x^a + y + y²` (or its block-swap), where `a = 3` (or `a = 9` on
Z_15, where `gcd(9, 15) = 3`). The x-axis support is `{(a, 0)}`,
which generates a subgroup of index `gcd(a, ℓ) > 1`. The y-axis
contribution `(0, 1), (0, 2)` only fills the y-direction. Together
the subgroup is `⟨(a, 0), (0, 1)⟩ = ⟨a⟩_ℓ × Z_m`, a proper subgroup
of `Z_ℓ × Z_m`.

| code_id | group | A | B | [G:⟨supp A⟩] | [G:⟨supp B⟩] | non-deg? |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---:|:---:|
| bb_72_12_6   | Z6xZ6  | `x^3 + y + y^2`   | `y^3 + x + x^2`   | 3 | 3 | **no** |
| bb_90_8_10   | Z15xZ3 | `x^9 + y + y^2`   | `1 + x^2 + x^7`   | 3 | 3 | **no** |
| bb_108_8_10  | Z9xZ6  | `x^3 + y + y^2`   | `y^3 + x + x^2`   | 3 | 3 | **no** |
| gross        | Z12xZ6 | `x^3 + y + y^2`   | `y^3 + x + x^2`   | 3 | 3 | **no** |
| bb_288_12_18 | Z12xZ12| `x^3 + y^2 + y^7` | `y^3 + x + x^2`   | 3 | 3 | **no** |

exactly what the conditional conjecture does, but the price is
losing the rest of the Bravyi family from the domain.

## 3. Alternative non-degeneracy definitions

The spec's definition is **strict per-polynomial**: each `⟨supp(A)⟩`
and `⟨supp(B)⟩` must equal `G`. Lin–Pryadko 2023 (§III.C, just before
Statement 11) and Kovalev-Pryadko 2013 implicitly use a **looser
joint definition**:

    NON-DEGENERATE_joint :≡  ⟨supp(A) ∪ supp(B)⟩ = G

i.e., the union of the two supports generates G. Under this joint
definition:

| code_id | |⟨supp(A)⟩| | |⟨supp(B)⟩| | |⟨supp(A) ∪ supp(B)⟩| | |G| | non-deg_joint? |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|:---:|
| bb_72_12_6   | 12 | 12 | 36 | 36 | **yes** |
| bb_90_8_10   | 15 | 15 | 45 | 45 | **yes** |
| bb_108_8_10  | 18 | 18 | 54 | 54 | **yes** |
| gross        | 24 | 24 | 72 | 72 | **yes** |
| bb_288_12_18 | 48 | 48 | 144 | 144 | **yes** |

Under the **joint** definition, all 5 Bravyi instances are
non-degenerate. Then the Bravyi-table verdict becomes:

| code_id | bound | d | slack | verdict_joint |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| bb_72_12_6 | 8 | 6 | -2 | **VIOLATION** (kills the conjecture) |
| bb_90_8_10 | 4 | 10 | +6 | OK (loose) |
| bb_108_8_10 | 4 | 10 | +6 | OK (loose) |
| gross | 8 | 12 | +4 | OK (loose) |
| bb_288_12_18 | 16 | 18 | +2 | OK (loose) |

The joint-definition version of the conditional conjecture is
**FALSIFIED on bb_72_12_6**. So neither definition gives a clean
conditional conjecture covering the Bravyi target:

* **Strict definition**: Bravyi out of scope. Whatever the conditional
  conjecture says, it doesn't help with the Bravyi-target codes.
* **Joint definition**: Bravyi in scope, but bb_72_12_6 violates.

---

## 4. Implication for Tier 4

A conditional bound that **excludes the engineering target** is
strategically useless: the whole point of this 4-tier program is to
prove a *tight or near-tight* bound on the gross polynomials. The
spec's strict non-degeneracy filter rules out the entire family of
codes that motivated the program.

A conditional bound that includes Bravyi (joint definition) is
**falsified** by bb_72_12_6.

Either way: the conditional Jacobson-radical conjecture is not a
viable Tier-4 candidate as-is. See `result.md` for the verdict.
