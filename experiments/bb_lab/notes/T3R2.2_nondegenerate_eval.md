# T3R2.2 — Real Jacobson bound on non-degenerate BB corpus

Tier-3 round 2 evaluates the **conditional** Jacobson-radical conjecture

> For non-degenerate BB codes,
> `d_X(BB(G, A, B)) ≥ Σ_{O ∈ V_A ∩ V_B} |O| · min(μ_O(A), μ_O(B))`
>
> where non-degenerate means `⟨supp(A)⟩ = G = ⟨supp(B)⟩`.

**Source data**: every corpus row with `d_exact` filled in.

## Headline numbers

- Total rows with `d_exact`: **3894**
- Non-degenerate rows: **1937** (49.7%)
- Degenerate rows: **1957** (50.3%)

### Violations of the *real* Jacobson bound

- Non-degenerate violations (d_exact < jacobson_bound): **43** / **1937** (2.2%)
- Degenerate violations: 364 / 1957 (18.6%)

### Proxy vs. real on the non-degenerate subset

- Proxy `d ≥ k/2` violations: 43 (2.2%)
- Real bound `d ≥ Σ|O|·μ_O` violations: 43 (2.2%)

The real bound is generally ≥ the proxy `k/2` since the latter is the
semisimple-quotient kernel count; the real bound adds Jacobson-radical depth.
